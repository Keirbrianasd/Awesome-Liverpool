## 💎 Hidden Gems

Liverpool rewards those who look beyond the obvious.

- [The Williamson Tunnels](https://www.williamsontunnels.co.uk) - A mysterious network of underground tunnels built in the early 19th century by eccentric merchant Joseph Williamson. Guided tours available.
- [The Crypt at St George's Hall](https://www.stgeorgeshallliverpool.co.uk) - Beneath the grand hall lies a stunning hidden crypt with beautiful columns and intricate stonework.
- [The Bluecoat](https://www.thebluecoat.org.uk) - Liverpool's oldest surviving building (1716), now a thriving arts centre with a hidden courtyard garden.
- [Sefton Park Secret Garden](https://liverpool.gov.uk/parks/sefton-park/) - Tucked within the grounds of Sefton Park, this tranquil walled garden is largely unknown even to locals.
- [The Oratory (St James's Cemetery)](https://www.liverpoolmuseums.org.uk) - A hidden sunken garden and cemetery beneath Liverpool Cathedral, completely peaceful and free to enter.
- [Liverpool's Chinatown Gate](https://www.visitliverpool.com/explore/chinatown) - Europe's biggest Chinese ceremonial arch - many Liverpudlians have never visited.
- [Stanley Dock Tobacco Warehouse](https://www.titanichotelliverpool.com) - The world's largest brick warehouse building.
- [The Philharmonic Pub Gents Toilets](https://nicholsonspubs.co.uk) - The ornate marble and mosaic Gents' toilets at The Phil are a listed attraction.
- [Everton Brow Viewpoint](https://liverpool.gov.uk/parks/everton-park/) - A hilltop vantage point in Everton Park with arguably the best panoramic view of Liverpool's skyline and the River Mersey.
